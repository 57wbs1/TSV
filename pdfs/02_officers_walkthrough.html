TSV App — Instruction Manual

The officer's walkthrough.

Everything a regular officer uses the app for — install, daily status updates, leaving the hotel, returning, calendar, rooms, map, ad-hoc messages, and settings. One read through and you'll know every tap you need.

Installing the app

The TSV app is a Progressive Web App. Install it once from your phone's browser; after that, it behaves like any other app on your home screen — own icon, full-screen, works on weak Wi-Fi, remembers your login. Works on both iPhone and Android.

iPhone · Safari

Open https://57wbs1.github.io/TSV/ in Safari. Chrome on iOS cannot install PWAs — only Safari can.
Tap the Share icon at the bottom of Safari (square with an arrow pointing up).
Scroll down in the share sheet and tap Add to Home Screen.
Confirm the name and tap Add. The TSV BKK icon appears on your home screen.

Android · Chrome

Open https://57wbs1.github.io/TSV/ in Chrome (or any Chromium browser — Edge, Samsung Internet, etc. all work).
Chrome usually shows a banner at the bottom: Add TSV BKK to Home screen. Tap it. If you don't see the banner, proceed to the next step.
Tap the ⋮ menu (top-right corner), then tap Install app. On older Chrome it may read Add to Home screen — same thing.
Confirm with Install. The icon appears in your app drawer and on your home screen.

First login · both platforms

Open the app from your home screen. Sign in with the PIN your admin issued you. On first login you'll be prompted to set your own PIN — pick something memorable, because the admin can reset it but will not see what you choose.

If the app won't install

  • iPhone: make sure you're in Safari, not Chrome or Firefox. If you tap Share and don't see Add to Home Screen, scroll further down the share sheet — iOS hides it below the share targets.
  • Android: if the ⋮ menu doesn't show Install app, Chrome probably needs to fully load the page first. Wait a few seconds, then retry. Private / incognito mode blocks PWA installs — switch to a normal tab.
  • Either platform: if the page shows a spinning circle and never loads, force-quit the browser or the app and reopen the link. The service worker is designed to recover on its second boot.

Parade state — update yourself by 0800H

Every officer defaults to Present each morning. If you are anything else — sick, hospitalised, absent from the trip, having a later start, whatever — you must update it in the app before 0800H. After that, your syndicate IC sees the roll in the command chat.

How to update

Tap the Tracker tab at the bottom.
Tap the Parade sub-tab near the top.
Find your row. Your syndicate shows first by default.
Tap the ✏️ Edit button on your row.
A prompt appears. Type your status — e.g. Sick in Hotel 27 Apr, Hospitalised, Absent from Trip UFN. Leave it as Present if nothing has changed.
Tap OK. The change saves immediately.
Important. The 0830H broadcast shows your status to your syndicate IC and leadership. It is not posted to all officers. Your IC is the person you need to have a correct picture of you.

Ad-hoc parade state — anyone can send

If an officer's status changes during the day (back from the clinic, joining late, whatever), anyone can push a fresh parade state to command without waiting for tomorrow's 0830H roll.

Tracker → Parade → 📤 Send Parade State.
Choose the syndicates to include, or leave the selection empty for a full roster send.
Add optional remarks such as All personnel accounted for or MAJ X back from clinic.
Tap Send.

Leaving the hotel — the buddy flow

Every time you step out of the hotel, log it. This is how command knows who is together, where, and how to reach you.

On the Home tab, tap 🚶 Leaving Hotel.
A sheet opens. Pick your syndicate from the dropdown.
The members of that syndicate appear as chips. Tap each officer you are walking out of the hotel with — they'll highlight as selected. This is the buddy system.
At the top of the sheet, type your location. Free-text, as specific as you like — e.g. Asok BTS — dinner, Terminal 21 food court, MBK — shopping.
Optional: tap Share GPS if you want your live location on the Map tab for admins. The app will ask for location permission the first time.
Tap confirm. Your status flips to OUT, your buddies flip to OUT with your location attached, and the syndicate list updates for everyone.

Buddy rules

  • Only tap buddies who are physically walking out with you. Not the friend upstairs who might join later.
  • If one of your buddies splits off and heads back to the hotel early, they should tap Return to Hotel on their own phone — don't do it for them.
  • GPS sharing is optional. If you skip it, your text location is still visible on the list view. GPS only adds a pin on the map.

Returning to the hotel — by 0200H

When you walk back through the hotel lobby, open the app and tap 🏨 Return to Hotel on the Home tab. That flips your status back to IN, stops any GPS sharing, and clears your buddy entry.

Why this matters. At 0200H the curfew SITREP fires. If you haven't tapped Return, you'll be listed as OUT and the Duty Officer will call you. Save them the trouble — a single tap when you enter the lobby is enough.

Ad-hoc SITREP — when movement changes

Scheduled SITREPs fire at 2300H and 0200H automatically. If something changes during the day — a bus delays, half your syndicate splits off, an officer leaves a visit early — a Syndicate IC can push a fresh SITREP, and if an IC is not reachable, so can you.

Tracker tab → List sub-tab (the default view).
Tap 📤 Send SITREP at the top.
Choose the syndicates to include.
Optional remarks — e.g. Syn 3 split at Wat Pho, 8 returning, 6 onward to MBK.
Send. The SITREP lands in the ops chat.

Calendar, transport, and reflections

Calendar sub-tab

Opens the day-by-day programme. Scroll through visits, timings, attire, and locations. Tap any event to expand the details — programme notes, points of contact, what to bring.

Transport sub-tab

Shows your flight and bus allocations. Driver names, bus numbers, timings, pickup points. Everything you need to find the right vehicle.

Reflections sub-tab

The nightly reflection form. Four fields: Key Observations, Patterns & Hypothesis, Implications for Singapore, Ah-Ha Moments. Fill in as many as apply and submit — the form feeds the cohort's learning debrief.

Map and Rooms Tracker

Map

Tracker → Map. Shows visit pins, SCOPE engagement pins, and live GPS for any officer currently sharing. Tap a pin for details.

Rooms Tracker

Tracker → Rooms. Update your hotel room number once. Your syndicate IC needs it to find you physically in an emergency.

Tracker → Rooms.
Find your name in the list.
Tap your row and enter your room number — e.g. 1408.
Save. You are done for the trip.

Settings

All personal controls sit in the Settings tab. Use it for:

  • PIN. Rotate the default PIN you were issued. Pick something memorable.
  • Display name. Fix typos or decide how you want to appear in SITREPs.
  • Text size and theme. Larger text, light or dark theme, layout adjustments for older iPhones.
  • Watermarks. Toggle the syndicate watermark on the home screen if you find it distracting.
  • Report an issue. Something broken or confusing? Open Settings, tap Report, type it, send. It reaches the admin directly.

The three things that matter most

If you only remember three things from this document:

  1. Update parade state by 0800H if you are not Present.
  2. Log every exit from the hotel with buddies and a location, and tap Return to Hotel on your way back in.
  3. Use the Settings feedback link to flag anything confusing or broken. The admin can fix it overnight.
